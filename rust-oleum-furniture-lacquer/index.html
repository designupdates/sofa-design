FAQ

Question 1: Can I use Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer on my outdoor garden furniture?
Answer 1: Yes, Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer is suitable for both indoor and outdoor use. Its durable formula can withstand the elements, providing excellent protection against rain, sunlight, and UV fading.

Question 2: How do I prepare my garden furniture before applying Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer?
Answer 2: To ensure the best results, make sure to clean your garden furniture thoroughly to remove any dirt, grease, or old finishes. Sand the surface lightly to create a smooth base for the lacquer to adhere to.

Question 3: How many coats of Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer should I apply?
Answer 3: For optimal protection and a long-lasting finish, we recommend applying 2-3 thin coats of Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer. Allow each coat to dry completely before applying the next.

Question 4: How long does it take for Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer to dry?
Answer 4: Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer dries quickly, allowing you to apply multiple coats in a single day. The lacquer dries to the touch in as little as 20 minutes and is fully cured in 24 hours.

Question 5: Can I use Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer on metal garden furniture?
Answer 5: Yes, Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer can be used on metal garden furniture. However, we recommend using a metal primer before applying the lacquer to ensure proper adhesion.

Question 6: How do I care for my garden furniture after applying Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer?

Tips

Here are some practical tips to help you achieve the best results when using Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer on your garden furniture:

Tip 1: Choose the right finish:
Consider the overall style of your garden and the desired look you want to achieve. Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer offers a variety of finishes, including gloss, semi-gloss, satin, and matte. For a classic and elegant look, choose a high-gloss finish. For a more relaxed and casual feel, opt for a satin or matte finish.

Tip 2: Prepare your furniture properly:
Proper preparation is key to a smooth and long-lasting finish. Make sure to clean your garden furniture thoroughly to remove any dirt, grease, or old finishes. Sand the surface lightly with fine-grit sandpaper to create a smooth base for the lacquer to adhere to.

Tip 3: Apply thin coats:
When applying Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer, it's better to apply several thin coats rather than one thick coat. This will help to prevent runs and drips, and it will also ensure that the lacquer dries evenly. Allow each coat to dry completely before applying the next.

Tip 4: Protect your furniture from the elements:
Once you've applied Rust-Oleum Furniture Lacquer to your garden furniture, it's important to protect it from the elements. If your furniture will be exposed to rain or sunlight, consider using a sealant or UV protectant to provide additional protection.

By following these tips, you can ensure that your garden furniture looks its best and lasts for many years to come.
