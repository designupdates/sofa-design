The history of Mason furniture dates back to the 17th century, when master craftsmen in the English countryside began creating pieces that were both beautiful and functional. These early pieces were often made from oak, a dense and durable wood that could withstand the rigors of daily use. Over time, Mason furniture became increasingly popular, and by the 19th century, it was being exported to all corners of the world.

Classic designs

Mason furniture is renowned for its classic designs, which have stood the test of time and continue to grace homes around the world. These timeless pieces are inspired by the finest traditions of English furniture making, with an emphasis on clean lines, elegant proportions, and intricate details.

One of the most iconic Mason furniture designs is the Chesterfield sofa. This classic piece is characterized by its deep button tufting, rolled arms, and low profile. The Chesterfield sofa is available in a wide range of fabrics and leathers, making it a versatile choice for any décor.

Another popular Mason furniture design is the wingback chair. This chair is known for its high back and wings, which provide both comfort and privacy. Wingback chairs are often upholstered in rich fabrics, such as velvet or leather, and make a stately addition to any room.

In addition to these iconic designs, Mason furniture also offers a wide range of other classic pieces, including dining tables, chairs, beds, and cabinets. These pieces are all crafted from the finest materials and feature the same attention to detail that is synonymous with the Mason name.

Whether you are looking for a single piece to add a touch of elegance to your home or a complete set of furniture to furnish your entire living space, Mason furniture is sure to have the perfect pieces for you.

Sturdy construction

Mason furniture is renowned for its sturdy construction, which ensures that it will last for generations to come. This durability is achieved through the use of the finest materials and traditional joinery techniques.

  • Solid wood frames

    All Mason furniture frames are made from solid wood, which is much stronger and more durable than particle board or MDF. The wood is carefully selected and seasoned to ensure that it is free from defects.

  • Mortise and tenon joinery

    Mason furniture uses mortise and tenon joinery, which is a traditional technique that creates a very strong and durable joint. The mortise is a hole cut into one piece of wood, and the tenon is a tongue-shaped piece of wood that fits into the mortise. The joint is then secured with glue and pegs.

  • Corner blocks

    Mason furniture frames are also reinforced with corner blocks, which are triangular pieces of wood that are glued and screwed into the corners of the frame. This provides additional strength and stability to the frame.

  • Metal hardware

    Mason furniture uses high-quality metal hardware, such as screws, bolts, and hinges. This hardware is designed to be strong and durable, and it will not rust or corrode over time.

As a result of its sturdy construction, Mason furniture is able to withstand the rigors of everyday use. It is also resistant to damage from moisture, heat, and cold. With proper care, Mason furniture will last for generations to come.

Intricate carvings

Mason furniture is renowned for its intricate carvings, which add a touch of elegance and sophistication to any piece. These carvings are hand-carved by skilled artisans, and they often feature complex patterns and motifs.

  • Acanthus leaves

    Acanthus leaves are a common motif in Mason furniture carvings. These leaves are characterized by their deeply lobed edges and serrated margins. They are often used to decorate the arms, legs, and backs of chairs, sofas, and other pieces of furniture.

  • Scrollwork

    Scrollwork is another popular motif in Mason furniture carvings. This type of carving features flowing, curved lines that create a sense of movement and grace. Scrollwork is often used to decorate the aprons of tables, the headboards of beds, and the frames of mirrors.

  • Garlands

    Garlands are a type of carving that features a series of flowers, leaves, and fruit strung together. Garlands are often used to decorate the tops of cabinets, the backs of chairs, and the frames of mirrors. They add a touch of whimsy and charm to any piece of furniture.

  • Figurative carvings

    Mason furniture also features a variety of figurative carvings, such as animals, birds, and human figures. These carvings are often used to decorate the arms of chairs, the legs of tables, and the finials of bedposts. They add a touch of personality and uniqueness to any piece of furniture.

The intricate carvings on Mason furniture are a testament to the skill and artistry of the craftsmen who create them. These carvings add a touch of elegance and sophistication to any piece of furniture, and they are sure to be admired for generations to come.
