Art Deco Furniture Tables: A Guide to Styles and Design

Written by Daniel Jul 31, 2029 · 21 min read

Art Deco furniture tables are a distinctive and stylish addition to any home. Characterized by bold geometric shapes, rich materials, and intricate details, these tables are works of art as well as functional pieces of furniture. Whether you're looking to add a touch of glamour to your living room or create a sophisticated workspace in your home office, an Art Deco furniture table is sure to make a statement.

In this guide, we'll explore the different styles of Art Deco furniture tables, from the sleek and simple to the more elaborate and ornate. We'll also discuss the materials used to create these tables, as well as the different types of finishes available. By the end of this guide, you'll be able to choose the perfect Art Deco furniture table for your home.

The history of Art Deco furniture tables can be traced back to the early 1900s, when the Art Deco style emerged as a reaction to the more traditional and ornate styles of the Victorian and Edwardian eras. Art Deco furniture is characterized by its clean lines, geometric shapes, and use of bold colors and materials. This style was inspired by the Art Deco movement, which was a style of architecture and design that was popular in the 1920s and 1930s. Art Deco furniture is often made from exotic materials such as ebony, ivory, and tortoiseshell. It is also often decorated with intricate carvings, marquetry, and metalwork.

Bold geometric shapes

One of the most distinctive features of Art Deco furniture tables is their bold geometric shapes. These shapes are often inspired by the architecture and design of the Art Deco period, which was characterized by its clean lines and geometric forms. Art Deco furniture tables often feature sharp angles, curves, and zigzags, which create a sense of movement and energy.

  • Rectangles: Rectangles are one of the most common geometric shapes used in Art Deco furniture tables. They can be used to create a variety of different looks, from simple and understated to more elaborate and ornate.

    For example, a simple rectangular table with clean lines and a polished finish would be a great choice for a modern or contemporary home. A more elaborate rectangular table with carved details and a metallic finish would be a great choice for a more traditional or formal home.

  • Circles: Circles are another common geometric shape used in Art Deco furniture tables. They can be used to create a sense of movement and energy, or to add a touch of whimsy to a space.

    For example, a round table with a curved base and a glass top would be a great choice for a small space or a breakfast nook. A round table with a carved wooden base and a marble top would be a great choice for a more formal dining room.

  • Triangles: Triangles are another geometric shape that is often used in Art Deco furniture tables. They can be used to create a sense of drama and excitement, or to add a touch of sophistication to a space.

    For example, a triangular table with a sharp angled base and a glass top would be a great choice for a modern or contemporary home. A triangular table with a carved wooden base and a marble top would be a great choice for a more traditional or formal home.

  • Zigzags: Zigzags are a type of geometric pattern that is often used in Art Deco furniture tables. They can be used to create a sense of movement and energy, or to add a touch of excitement to a space.

    For example, a table with a zigzag pattern on the base or the top would be a great choice for a modern or contemporary home. A table with a zigzag pattern on the legs or the apron would be a great choice for a more traditional or formal home.

These are just a few of the many different geometric shapes that can be used in Art Deco furniture tables. When choosing a table, it is important to consider the overall style of your home and the desired effect you want to create.

Question 1: What are the different types of garden furniture?

Answer 3: When choosing garden furniture, it is important to consider the size of your space. Question 4: How do I care for my garden furniture?
Answer 4: The best way to care for your garden furniture is to clean it regularly. This will help to prevent dirt and grime from building up, which can damage the furniture over time. If your furniture is made of wood, you should also apply a sealant to protect it from the sun and rain.

Question 5: Can I leave my garden furniture outside all year round?
Answer 5: Whether or not you can leave your garden furniture outside all year round depends on the material it is made from.
