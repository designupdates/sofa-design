How to Restore French Polished Furniture

Written by Eveline Aug 17, 2025 Â· 13 min read

If you're lucky enough to own a piece of French polished furniture, you know that it's a thing of beauty. French polishing is a traditional technique that creates a deep, glossy finish that is both elegant and timeless. However, over time, even the most well-cared-for French polished furniture can start to show signs of wear and tear.

If your French polished furniture is looking a little tired, don't despair. With a little care and attention, you can restore it to its former glory. Here are a few tips on how to restore French polished furniture:

Before you begin any restoration work, it's important to assess the condition of the furniture. If the finish is only slightly scratched or dull, you may be able to restore it with a simple cleaning and polishing. However, if the finish is badly damaged, you may need to refinish the furniture completely.

How to Restore French Polished Furniture

Follow these steps to restore your French polished furniture to its former glory:

  • Assess the damage.
  • Clean the furniture.
  • Repair any damage.
  • Apply a fresh coat of polish.
  • Buff the furniture to a shine.
  • Protect the finish.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals.
  • Dust regularly.
  • Store furniture in a climate-controlled environment.

With a little care and attention, you can keep your French polished furniture looking its best for years to come.

Assess the damage.

The first step in restoring French polished furniture is to assess the damage. This will help you determine the best course of action.

  • Check for scratches.

    Scratches are the most common type of damage to French polished furniture. They can be caused by everyday use, accidents, or improper cleaning.

  • Look for dents.

    Dents are another common type of damage to French polished furniture. They can be caused by bumps, drops, or pressure.

  • Examine the finish.

    The finish of French polished furniture can become dull or faded over time. This can be caused by exposure to sunlight, heat, or harsh chemicals.

  • Check for cracks.

    Cracks in the finish of French polished furniture are a serious problem. They can be caused by structural damage or by improper restoration attempts.

Once you have assessed the damage to your French polished furniture, you can determine the best course of action. If the damage is minor, you may be able to restore the furniture yourself. However, if the damage is severe, you may need to hire a professional.

Clean the furniture.

Once you have assessed the damage to your French polished furniture, the next step is to clean it. This will remove any dirt, dust, or grime that may be obscuring the finish.

To clean French polished furniture, you will need:

  • A soft, lint-free cloth
  • A mild dish soap
  • Warm water

Instructions:

  1. Dampen the cloth with warm water and a small amount of dish soap.
  2. Gently wipe the furniture with the damp cloth, following the grain of the wood.
  3. Be sure to get into all the nooks and crannies.
  4. Rinse the cloth frequently in clean water.
  5. Dry the furniture thoroughly with a clean, dry cloth.

Once you have cleaned the furniture, you can assess the damage more closely. If there are any scratches or dents, you can repair them before applying a fresh coat of polish.

Tips:

  • Do not use harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners on French polished furniture.
  • Do not use too much water when cleaning the furniture. This can damage the finish.
  • Be sure to dry the furniture thoroughly after cleaning.

Repair any damage.

Once you have cleaned the French polished furniture, you can repair any damage. This may include scratches, dents, or cracks.

  • Repair scratches.

    Small scratches can be repaired with a furniture scratch repair kit. These kits typically contain a filler stick and a touch-up marker. Follow the instructions on the kit to repair the scratches.

  • Repair dents.

    Dents can be repaired with a furniture dent repair kit. These kits typically contain a filler compound and a putty knife. Follow the instructions on the kit to repair the dents.

  • Repair cracks.

    Cracks in the finish of French polished furniture are a serious problem. They can be repaired by a professional furniture restorer. Do not attempt to repair cracks yourself, as this could further damage the furniture.

  • Re-glue loose joints.

    If any of the joints in the furniture are loose, you can re-glue them using a wood glue. Be sure to use a glue that is specifically designed for furniture.

Once you have repaired any damage to the furniture, you can apply a fresh coat of polish.

Apply a fresh coat of polish.

Once you have repaired any damage to the French polished furniture, you can apply a fresh coat of polish. This will help to protect the finish and give the furniture a beautiful shine.

To apply a fresh coat of polish, you will need:

  • French polish
  • A soft, lint-free cloth
  • A polishing pad

Instructions:

  1. Apply a small amount of French polish to the cloth.
  2. Rub the cloth over the furniture in a circular motion, following the grain of the wood.
  3. Be sure to apply the polish evenly.
  4. Once you have applied the polish to the entire piece of furniture, buff it with the polishing pad.
  5. This will help to remove any excess polish and give the furniture a beautiful shine.

You can apply multiple coats of polish for a more durable finish. However, be sure to allow each coat to dry completely before applying the next.

Tips:

  • Use a high-quality French polish that is specifically designed for furniture.
  • Apply the polish in a well-ventilated area.
  • Be sure to wear gloves when applying the polish.
  • Do not apply the polish to the furniture in direct sunlight.

Buff the furniture to a shine.

Once you have applied a fresh coat of polish to the French polished furniture, you can buff it to a shine. This will help to remove any excess polish and give the furniture a beautiful, glossy finish.

  • Use a soft, lint-free cloth.

    A soft, lint-free cloth will help to remove any excess polish and buff the furniture to a shine without scratching the finish.

  • Buff the furniture in a circular motion.

    Buff the furniture in a circular motion, following the grain of the wood. This will help to distribute the polish evenly and give the furniture a uniform shine.

  • Apply pressure.

    Apply gentle pressure as you buff the furniture. This will help to remove any excess polish and bring out the shine of the finish.

  • Continue buffing until the furniture is shiny.

    Continue buffing the furniture until it is shiny and has a uniform finish. This may take some time, but it is worth it to achieve a beautiful, glossy finish.

Tips:

  • You can also use a polishing pad to buff the furniture. However, be sure to use a pad that is specifically designed for French polished furniture.
  • If the furniture is very dirty or has a lot of scratches, you may need to apply multiple coats of polish and buff the furniture in between each coat.
  • Once you have buffed the furniture to a shine, you can protect the finish by applying a coat of wax.

Protect the finish.

Once you have restored the French polished furniture to its former glory, you need to protect the finish to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use coasters and placemats.

    Coasters and placemats will help to protect the finish of the furniture from scratches and stains.

  • Avoid placing the furniture in direct sunlight.

    Direct sunlight can damage the finish of the furniture over time.

  • Keep the furniture away from heat sources.

    Heat sources, such as fireplaces and radiators, can also damage the finish of the furniture.

  • Dust the furniture regularly.

    Dusting the furniture regularly will help to remove any dirt or debris that could scratch the finish.

Additional tips:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners on the furniture.
  • If you need to clean the furniture, use a mild dish soap and warm water.
  • Dry the furniture thoroughly after cleaning.
  • Apply a coat of wax to the furniture every few months to protect the finish.

Avoid harsh chemicals.

Harsh chemicals can damage the finish of French polished furniture. This includes cleaners, polishes, and waxes that contain harsh ingredients. Harsh chemicals can strip the finish from the furniture, leaving it dull and lifeless.

  • Read the labels of cleaning products carefully.

    Before you use any cleaning product on French polished furniture, read the label carefully to make sure that it does not contain any harsh chemicals.

  • Avoid using abrasive cleaners.

    Abrasive cleaners, such as scouring powders and steel wool, can scratch the finish of French polished furniture.

  • Avoid using ammonia-based cleaners.

    Ammonia-based cleaners can damage the finish of French polished furniture.

  • Avoid using citrus-based cleaners.

    Citrus-based cleaners can also damage the finish of French polished furniture.

If you are unsure whether a cleaning product is safe to use on French polished furniture, it is best to err on the side of caution and avoid using it.

Dust regularly.

Dust is one of the biggest enemies of French polished furniture. Dust can scratch the finish and make it look dull and lifeless. Dusting the furniture regularly will help to keep it looking its best.

  • Use a soft, lint-free cloth.

    A soft, lint-free cloth will help to remove dust without scratching the finish.

  • Dust the furniture in the direction of the grain.

    Dusting the furniture in the direction of the grain will help to prevent scratches.

  • Be sure to get into all the nooks and crannies.

    Dust can accumulate in the nooks and crannies of the furniture, so be sure to get into these areas when you are dusting.

  • Dust the furniture at least once a week.

    Dusting the furniture at least once a week will help to keep it looking its best.

Tips:

  • You can also use a vacuum cleaner with a soft brush attachment to dust the furniture.
  • If the furniture is very dusty, you may need to use a slightly damp cloth to remove the dust.
  • Be sure to dry the furniture thoroughly after dusting.

Store furniture in a climate-controlled environment.

French polished furniture is best stored in a climate-controlled environment. This means a room or space where the temperature and humidity are relatively constant. Ideal conditions for storing French polished furniture are a temperature between 65 and 75 degrees Fahrenheit and a relative humidity between 45 and 55 percent.

Why is it important to store French polished furniture in a climate-controlled environment?

  • Extreme temperatures can damage the finish.

    Extreme temperatures, both hot and cold, can cause the finish of French polished furniture to crack or peel.

  • High humidity can cause the furniture to swell.

    High humidity can cause the wood of French polished furniture to swell, which can lead to cracks and other damage.

  • Low humidity can cause the furniture to dry out.

    Low humidity can cause the wood of French polished furniture to dry out, which can lead to cracks and other damage.

Tips for storing French polished furniture in a climate-controlled environment:

  • If you live in a climate with extreme temperatures, consider storing your French polished furniture in a climate-controlled storage facility.
  • If you store your French polished furniture in a room or space that is not climate-controlled, use a humidifier or dehumidifier to maintain the proper humidity level.
  • Avoid placing your French polished furniture near heat sources, such as fireplaces and radiators.
  • Avoid placing your French polished furniture in direct sunlight.

By following these tips, you can help to protect your French polished furniture and keep it looking its best for years to come.
