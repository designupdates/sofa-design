Safety: Avoid furniture with sharp edges or loose parts that could be dangerous for your cat.

When choosing white cat furniture, it's important to avoid furniture with sharp edges or loose parts that could be dangerous for your cat. Here are a few things to look for:

Sharp edges: Avoid furniture with sharp edges that could cut or scratch your cat. This includes furniture with metal trim or sharp corners.

Loose parts: Avoid furniture with loose parts that your cat could chew on or swallow. This includes furniture with buttons, beads, or other small pieces that could be a choking hazard.

Unstable furniture: Avoid furniture that is unstable or wobbly. This includes furniture that is not properly assembled or that has loose legs. Unstable furniture can tip over and injure your cat.

Furniture with cords: Avoid furniture with electrical cords that your cat could chew on. This includes furniture with lamps, fans, or other electrical devices. Chewing on electrical cords can be dangerous for your cat and can also damage the furniture.

By following these tips, you can choose white cat furniture that is safe and comfortable for your furry friend.
