Reclaimed timber origins

Nathan Furniture's early years were marked by its innovative use of reclaimed timber. This unique approach to furniture making not only set the company apart from its competitors but also reflected Nathan Hurwitz's commitment to sustainability and resourcefulness.

  • Post-War Scarcity:

  • Sourcing Reclaimed Timber:

    Nathan Hurwitz scoured the streets of London, collecting discarded timber from demolished buildings and construction sites. This reclaimed timber, often weathered and worn, possessed a unique character and charm.

  • Transforming Timber into Furniture:

    With his skilled craftsmanship and keen eye for design, Nathan Hurwitz transformed these reclaimed timber pieces into beautiful and functional furniture. He carefully selected each piece of timber, highlighting its natural grain and imperfections.

  • Sustainable Ethos:

    Nathan Furniture's use of reclaimed timber was not only a practical solution to the post-war material shortage but also a reflection of Nathan Hurwitz's commitment to sustainability. By reusing and repurposing discarded timber, he was reducing waste and promoting a more circular economy.

Nathan Furniture's early focus on reclaimed timber laid the foundation for its reputation as a company that values sustainability and innovation. Even today, the company continues to incorporate reclaimed and recycled materials into its furniture designs, demonstrating its commitment to environmental responsibility.

Time-honored manufacturing techniques

Nathan Furniture employs time-honored manufacturing techniques to create furniture pieces that are both beautiful and durable. These techniques have been passed down through generations of furniture makers and ensure that each piece of Nathan furniture is made to the highest standards.

Here are some of the key time-honored manufacturing techniques used by Nathan Furniture:

  • Hand-Carving: Nathan Furniture's skilled craftsmen use hand-carving techniques to create intricate details and embellishments on furniture pieces. This traditional technique adds a unique and personal touch to each piece.
  • Dovetail Joinery: Nathan Furniture uses dovetail joinery to connect the different parts of its furniture pieces. This strong and durable joint ensures that furniture pieces will withstand the test of time.
  • Mortise and Tenon Joinery: Mortise and tenon joinery is another traditional technique used by Nathan Furniture. This joint is created by inserting a tenon (a protruding piece of wood) into a mortise (a hole). This creates a strong and secure connection between two pieces of wood.
  • Hand-Finishing: Nathan Furniture's furniture pieces are hand-finished to ensure a smooth and flawless surface. This process involves sanding, staining, and polishing the wood to bring out its natural beauty.

Nathan Furniture's commitment to using time-honored manufacturing techniques ensures that its furniture pieces are not only aesthetically pleasing but also durable and long-lasting. These techniques have been proven over centuries to produce furniture of exceptional quality, and Nathan Furniture is proud to carry on this tradition.

FAQ

Have questions about garden furniture? Here are some frequently asked questions and answers to help you choose, care for, and enjoy your outdoor furniture.

Question 1: What are the different types of garden furniture materials?
Answer 1: Garden furniture is typically made from a variety of materials, including wood (teak, acacia, eucalyptus), metal (aluminum, wrought iron, stainless steel), plastic (polyethylene, polyresin, PVC), and wicker (natural, synthetic).

Question 2: How do I choose the right garden furniture for my needs?
Answer 2: Consider factors such as the size of your garden, the intended use of the furniture (dining, lounging, entertaining), your personal style preferences, and the local climate.

Question 3: How do I care for my garden furniture?
Answer 3: Follow the care instructions provided by the manufacturer. Regular cleaning and maintenance will help to extend the life of your garden furniture.

Question 4: How can I protect my garden furniture from the elements?
Answer 4: Use furniture covers or store your furniture indoors during harsh weather conditions. Applying protective sealants or finishes can also help to protect your furniture from the sun and rain.

Question 5: Can I leave my garden furniture outdoors all year round?
Answer 5: Some garden furniture materials, such as teak and aluminum, can withstand outdoor exposure. However, it's generally recommended to store furniture indoors or use protective covers during extreme weather conditions.

Question 6: Where can I buy garden furniture?
Answer 6: Garden furniture is available at a variety of retailers, including furniture stores, home improvement stores, and online retailers.

Question 7: What are some tips for creating a stylish and comfortable garden space?
Answer 7: Arrange your furniture to create conversation areas and traffic flow. Add cushions, throws, and other accessories to make your furniture more comfortable and inviting. Incorporate plants and flowers to create a natural and inviting atmosphere.

With careful consideration and care, your garden furniture can provide you with years of enjoyment and relaxation.

Tips

Here are a few practical tips to help you choose, arrange, and care for your garden furniture, creating a stylish and inviting outdoor space:

Tip 1: Choose the Right Materials:
Consider the local climate and your maintenance preferences when selecting garden furniture materials. Teak and aluminum are durable and weather-resistant, while plastic and wicker require less maintenance. Choose materials that suit your needs and lifestyle.

Tip 2: Arrange for Comfort and Functionality:
Arrange your garden furniture to create comfortable conversation areas and ensure smooth traffic flow. Place seating near focal points like a garden view or a water feature. Consider adding a mix of seating options, such as chairs, benches, and ottomans, to accommodate different needs.

Tip 3: Accessorize for Style and Comfort:
Add cushions, throws, and other accessories to make your garden furniture more comfortable and inviting. Choose weather-resistant fabrics and materials that can withstand outdoor conditions. Incorporate outdoor rugs, lanterns, and potted plants to create a cozy and stylish atmosphere.

Tip 4: Care and Maintenance:
Regular cleaning and maintenance will extend the life of your garden furniture. Follow the care instructions provided by the manufacturer. Clean your furniture with mild soap and water, and apply protective sealants or finishes as recommended. Store furniture indoors or use protective covers during harsh weather conditions.

By following these tips, you can create a stylish and functional garden space that invites relaxation and enjoyment.
