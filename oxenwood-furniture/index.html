Traditional techniques employed

Oxenwood Furniture is committed to preserving traditional woodworking techniques that have been passed down through generations. These techniques have been refined over centuries and result in furniture that is both beautiful and durable.

  • Dovetail joinery

    Dovetail joinery is a strong and durable method of connecting two pieces of wood. Oxenwood's artisans use dovetail joinery in the construction of their drawers and other furniture components. This technique ensures that the joints will remain strong and secure over time.

  • Mortise and tenon joinery

    Mortise and tenon joinery is another traditional technique used by Oxenwood's artisans. This technique involves creating a mortise (a hole) in one piece of wood and a tenon (a protruding tongue) on another piece of wood. The tenon is then inserted into the mortise, creating a strong and durable joint.

  • Hand-cut dovetails

    Oxenwood's artisans use hand-cut dovetails to create beautiful and precise joints. This technique requires a high level of skill and experience, and the results are evident in the quality of Oxenwood furniture.

  • French polishing

    French polishing is a traditional technique used to create a beautiful and durable finish on Oxenwood furniture. This technique involves applying multiple layers of shellac to the wood, each layer being hand-rubbed to create a smooth and glossy finish.

Oxenwood's commitment to traditional techniques ensures that their furniture is not only beautiful but also built to last. These techniques have been proven over centuries to produce furniture that is both durable and stylish.

Answer: The best way to care for your garden furniture is to clean it regularly with a mild soap and water solution. You should also protect it from the elements by covering it when not in use.
