FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about storing garden furniture:

Question 1: What is the best way to store plastic garden furniture?
Answer: Plastic garden furniture is durable and can withstand most weather conditions. However, it is best to store it in a dry and shaded area during the winter months to protect it from fading and cracking. Question 2: How should I store wooden garden furniture?
Answer: Wooden garden furniture requires more care and attention during storage. It should be cleaned and dried thoroughly before storing. Apply a coat of furniture oil or wax to protect the wood from moisture and insects. Store wooden furniture in a dry and well-ventilated area. Question 3: Can I store metal garden furniture outdoors?
Answer: Yes, metal garden furniture is rust-resistant and can be stored outdoors. However, it is best to cover it with a tarp or furniture cover to protect it from harsh weather conditions. Question 4: How do I store wicker garden furniture?
Answer: Wicker garden furniture is delicate and should be stored in a dry and dust-free area. Wrap wicker furniture in a breathable fabric, such as a cotton sheet, to protect it from dust and moisture. Question 5: What is the best way to store rattan garden furniture?
Answer: Rattan garden furniture is similar to wicker furniture and requires similar storage conditions. Store rattan furniture in a dry and well-ventilated area, and cover it with a breathable fabric to protect it from dust and moisture. Question 6: Can I store fabric garden furniture outdoors?
Answer: No, fabric garden furniture should not be stored outdoors. Fabric can easily mildew and mold in damp conditions. Store fabric furniture in a dry and well-ventilated area.

Tips

Here are some practical tips for storing garden furniture:

Tip 1: Clean and dry your furniture before storing it. This will help to prevent mold and mildew from growing on your furniture.

Tip 2: Cover your furniture with a tarp or furniture cover. This will protect your furniture from dust, dirt, and moisture.

Tip 3: Store your furniture in a dry and well-ventilated area. This will help to prevent rust and corrosion on metal furniture and mold and mildew on wooden furniture.

Tip 4: Elevate your furniture off the ground. This will help to prevent moisture from wicking into your furniture and causing damage.
